Ontology-Aware DeepFPLearn Platform

Automatic and Explainable PFAS Toxicity Prediction and Environmental Decision Support

Platform Purpose

The OA-DeepFPLearn Platform combines molecular fingerprints, ontology-based knowledge engineering, Node2Vec embeddings, and explainable artificial intelligence to automatically predict PFAS toxicity, generate risk assessments, provide ontology reasoning paths, and support environmental decision making.

System Workflow

SMILES → RDKit → Compound Identification → PFAS Classification → Ontology Graph Lookup → Node2Vec Embedding → OA‑DeepFPLearn → Explainability Engine → Risk Assessment → Regulatory Recommendation

Compound Identification

Automatically identifies PFAS-like compounds from SMILES structures and determines likely chemical classes.

Ontology Intelligence

Uses a PFAS knowledge graph containing chemical classes, toxicity endpoints, environmental pathways, risk levels, and regulatory concepts.

Prediction Engine

Combines molecular fingerprints and ontology embeddings to estimate toxicity probability and risk category.

Explainability

Provides ontology reasoning paths, semantic explanations, attention-based interpretation, and recommended actions.

Outputs

  • Toxicity Probability
  • Predicted Toxic / Non‑Toxic Status
  • Risk Category
  • Compound Information
  • Ontology Reasoning Path
  • Regulatory Status
  • Recommended Actions

Supported Models

Encoder‑DeepFPLearn, GNN‑DeepFPLearn, GAT‑DeepFPLearn, GraphSAGE‑DeepFPLearn, GraphSAGE++‑DeepFPLearn, and ChemBERTa‑DeepFPLearn.
